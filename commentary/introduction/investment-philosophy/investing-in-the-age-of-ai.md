# How does AI change investing?

The human brain is uniquely suited to dealing with wicked domains. It has evolved to be the ultimate generalist computing tool. In contrast, machine learnings models are fit to large amounts of data, and struggle once they encounter the slightest bit of uncertainty.

As such, I think it will be longer than people think until machine learning models are able to set prices. The way we design AI models today contrasts starkly to how the human brain has evolved.

In the meantime, AI is incredibly useful for summarizing large amounts of data, performing calculations, and explaining concepts. It is an incredibly useful tool for me that will continue to get better. But the human brain will still be necessary to make decisions. 
Sell-side researchers will still be needed (the models are trained on their content, after all). Buy-side researchers will still be needed to make differentiated decisions (I asked Claude to recommend me a stock, and it said Coca-cola).

There is going to be increasingly less value in being an Excel Ninja or PowerPoint monkey. Investors should focus more on their own unique strengths and what their brains can do that an AI program cannot.

In addition, I see the quant landscape becoming increasingly bifurcated. This space is likely to become increasingly winner take all.
